# Customer Balance & Financial Behavior Analysis
# -Group Work-

## Overview
This project analyzes customer data from a retail bank in Portugal to understand patterns in account balances, financial product usage, and potential credit risk. The goal was to identify characteristics of customers with low account balances and uncover insights that can support better business and marketing decisions.

## Business Problem
The bank observed an increasing number of customers maintaining balances close to or below zero. This raised concerns about customer financial health, product usage, and potential credit risk.

## Objective
The goal of this project was to:
- Identify characteristics of customers with low account balances (≤ $1000)
- Analyze relationships between balance, loans, and default risk
- Explore how customer demographics influence financial behavior
- Provide insights to support customer targeting and risk management

## Key Business Questions
- Is there a relationship between customer age and low account balances?
- Do customers with low balances already have loans?
- Are low-balance customers more likely to default?
- How does product usage relate to account balance?

## Tools & Technologies
- Python (Pandas, NumPy)
- Jupyter Notebook
- Data Visualization (Matplotlib / Seaborn)

## Data
The dataset includes customer demographic and financial variables such as:
- Age
- Account Balance
- Loan Status
- Housing Loan
- Default Status
- Financial Product Usage

## Analysis
- Cleaned and prepared customer data for analysis
- Performed exploratory data analysis (EDA)
- Segmented customers based on account balance
- Analyzed relationships between financial behavior and risk indicators
- Visualized trends and correlations across key variables

## Key Insights
- Customers with lower account balances showed distinct behavioral patterns compared to higher-balance customers
- Loan and credit behavior varied significantly across balance segments
- Certain demographic factors (such as age) were associated with financial outcomes
- Identified potential indicators of higher default risk

## Business Impact
This analysis can help the bank:
- Better target customers with low balances
- Identify at-risk customers earlier
- Improve marketing strategies for financial products
- Support data-driven decision-making in customer management
